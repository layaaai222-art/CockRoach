## 2. FEDERAL LOAN PROGRAMS

### 2.1 SBA 7(a) Loan Program
- **What it is:** The SBA's flagship loan program — for most business purposes (working capital, equipment, real estate)
- **Amount:** Up to $5 million
- **Terms:** Up to 10 years (working capital), 25 years (real estate)
- **Interest rates:** Prime + 2.25%–4.75% (capped by SBA)
- **Best for:** Established businesses (6+ months) with revenue; not ideal for pre-revenue startups
- **How to apply:** Through SBA-approved lenders (banks, credit unions, CDFIs)
- **Key requirement:** Must be a for-profit business, owner must have invested equity, must not qualify for other financing

### 2.2 SBA 504 Loan Program
- **What it is:** For major fixed assets — commercial real estate, heavy equipment
- **Amount:** Up to $5.5 million (SBA portion); total project can be larger
- **Best for:** Asset-heavy businesses (manufacturing, hospitality, retail with property)
- **Structure:** 50% bank + 40% SBA/CDC + 10% borrower
- **Not for:** Working capital, inventory, or software startups

### 2.3 SBA Microloan Program
- **What it is:** Small loans for early-stage businesses and startups
- **Amount:** Up to $50,000 (average loan ~$13,000)
- **Terms:** Up to 6 years
- **Best for:** Pre-revenue or early-revenue startups, women/minority/veteran founders
- **How to apply:** Through SBA-designated nonprofit intermediaries (not banks)
- **Additional benefit:** Many intermediaries also provide technical assistance and mentoring

### 2.4 SBA Express Loan
- **What it is:** Faster version of 7(a) — 36-hour turnaround on approval decision
- **Amount:** Up to $500,000
- **Best for:** Businesses needing quick access to capital with some operating history

## 3. FEDERAL GRANT PROGRAMS

### 3.1 SBIR — Small Business Innovation Research
- **What it is:** Competitive grant program for small businesses doing R&D with commercialization potential
- **Phases:**
  - Phase I: Up to $275,000 — feasibility study (6 months)
  - Phase II: Up to $1.83 million — full R&D development (2 years)
  - Phase III: Commercialization (no SBIR funds — private or non-SBIR federal)
- **Who issues it:** 11 federal agencies (DOD, NIH, NASA, DOE, NSF, etc.)
- **Eligibility:** US-owned, for-profit, <500 employees, principal researcher must be employed by company
- **Best for:** Deep tech, biotech, cleantech, defense tech, medical devices, AI research
- **Key resource:** sbir.gov — search by agency and topic
- **Cockroach note:** If the idea has any tech innovation angle, always surface SBIR as an option

### 3.2 STTR — Small Business Technology Transfer
- **What it is:** Similar to SBIR but requires partnership with a US research institution (university or federal lab)
- **Phases:** Same structure as SBIR
- **Best for:** Ideas born from or closely tied to academic research
- **Key difference from SBIR:** Researcher does NOT need to be primarily employed by the small business

### 3.3 NIH Grants
- **Relevant for:** Health, biotech, medical devices, mental health apps, health data platforms
- **Key programs:**
  - NIH SBIR/STTR (via NCI, NIMH, NIDDK, etc.)
  - R01 grants (typically academic, but some startups qualify)
  - Biomedical Translational Research Information System (BTRIS)
- **Resource:** grants.nih.gov

### 3.4 NSF Grants
- **Relevant for:** Deep tech, AI/ML, materials science, engineering, computer science
- **Key program:** NSF SBIR — specifically designed for science-based startups
- **America's Seed Fund:** NSF's branding for their startup grant program — up to $2M total
- **Resource:** seedfund.nsf.gov

### 3.5 DOE Grants
- **Relevant for:** Cleantech, energy efficiency, renewable energy, battery tech, grid technology
- **Key programs:**
  - DOE SBIR/STTR
  - ARPA-E (for transformative energy research — highly competitive)
  - Loan Programs Office (for larger energy projects)
- **Resource:** energy.gov/sbir

### 3.6 USDA Grants
- **Relevant for:** Agtech, food tech, rural businesses, sustainable agriculture, rural broadband
- **Key programs:**
  - SBIR program through USDA NIFA
  - Rural Business Development Grant (RBDG)
  - Value-Added Producer Grant (VAPG)
  - Rural Energy for America Program (REAP)
- **Resource:** usda.gov/topics/rural

### 3.7 IRS Section 1202 — QSBS (Qualified Small Business Stock)
- **What it is:** Federal tax exclusion — up to 100% capital gains exclusion on sale of qualifying startup stock
- **Amount:** Excludes up to $10 million (or 10× basis) in gains per investor per company
- **Eligibility:** C-Corp only, active business, gross assets under $50M at time of issuance, stock held 5+ years
- **Why it matters:** Makes investing in your company dramatically more attractive to angels
- **Cockroach note:** Always mention this when advising on entity structure for fundable startups — it's one of the most underutilized startup tax advantages

## 4. CERTIFICATION-BASED PROGRAMS & SMART MOVES

These certifications unlock specific contracting opportunities, grants, and financing programs that are legally set aside for qualifying businesses. This is where strategic thinking pays off.

### 4.1 WOSB — Women-Owned Small Business
- **What it unlocks:**
  - Federal contracting set-asides in underrepresented industries (set aside from larger competitors)
  - SBA's WOSB Federal Contracting Program
  - Access to SCORE mentoring networks
  - Multiple state-level grant programs
- **Eligibility:** 51%+ owned, controlled, and managed by women; small business per SBA size standards
- **How to certify:** Self-certification or third-party via SBA-approved certifiers
- **Smart move:** *"If you have a qualifying co-founder, partner, or family member, WOSB certification opens federal contract set-asides legally ring-fenced from larger competitors — a competitive moat most founders completely ignore."*

### 4.2 EDWOSB — Economically Disadvantaged WOSB
- **What it unlocks:** Additional set-asides beyond standard WOSB in more industries
- **Eligibility:** WOSB requirements + personal net worth under $850K (excluding primary residence and business interest)

### 4.3 MBE — Minority Business Enterprise
- **What it unlocks:**
  - Corporate supplier diversity programs (Fortune 500 companies have procurement targets)
  - MBDA (Minority Business Development Agency) programs and business centers
  - State and local set-aside contracts
  - Access to minority-focused venture capital and angel networks
- **Eligibility:** 51%+ owned by US citizens who are minority group members
- **Certifying body:** National Minority Supplier Development Council (NMSDC) for corporate programs; government agencies for government programs
- **Smart move:** *"Large corporations have supplier diversity mandates and often struggle to find qualified minority-owned vendors. MBE certification turns your demographic into a business development asset."*

### 4.4 VOSB / SDVOSB — Veteran-Owned Small Business
- **What it unlocks:**
  - VA contracting set-asides (Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned gets priority)
  - Boots to Business program (SBA + DoD entrepreneur training)
  - Veteran-focused accelerators and funding programs
  - Hirepurpose and similar hiring/BD networks
- **Eligibility:** 51%+ owned and controlled by veterans; SDVOSB requires service-connected disability
- **Certifying body:** VA for SDVOSB; SBA for VOSB

### 4.5 HUBZone — Historically Underutilized Business Zone
- **What it unlocks:**
  - 10% price preference on federal contract bids
  - Set-aside contracts in certain categories
  - Competitive advantage in federal procurement
- **Eligibility:** 51%+ owned by US citizens, principal office in a HUBZone, 35%+ of employees living in HUBZones
- **How to check:** SBA HUBZone map tool at sba.gov
- **Smart move:** *"If your office is in a qualifying zip code, HUBZone certification gives you a 10% price advantage on federal contracts — meaning you can bid 10% higher than competitors and still win."*

### 4.6 DBE — Disadvantaged Business Enterprise
- **What it unlocks:**
  - USDOT contracting opportunities
  - State DOT set-aside contracts (transportation projects)
  - Infrastructure project subcontracting
- **Eligibility:** Socially and economically disadvantaged individuals; 51%+ owned; personal net worth under $1.32M
- **Certifying body:** State DOT agencies
- **Best for:** Construction, engineering, transportation, infrastructure tech

### 4.7 B Corp Certification
- **What it unlocks:**
  - Access to impact investor networks (specific VCs and angels who mandate portfolio diversity)
  - Premium brand positioning with ESG-conscious customers and enterprise buyers
  - B Corp community network and business development
  - Easier access to ESG-focused institutional capital
- **Not government-issued:** Certified by B Lab (nonprofit)
- **Best for:** Consumer brands, social enterprises, sustainability-focused businesses
- **Smart move:** *"If your business model has any social/environmental angle, B Corp certification increasingly appears on enterprise procurement checklists — especially with European and ESG-mandated buyers."*

## 5. PRIVATE ACCELERATORS & PROGRAMS

### Tier 1 — Apply to These First
- **Y Combinator (YC):** $500K for 7% equity; most selective; best network in the world; batch twice yearly
- **Techstars:** $120K for 6%; vertical-specific programs; strong corporate partner network
- **a16z START:** Pre-seed program from Andreessen Horowitz; no equity taken for initial program
- **Sequoia Arc:** Founder development program; highly selective

### Tier 2 — Strong Programs
- **500 Global:** $150K for 6%; global network; strong for international expansion
- **First Round Fast Track:** Pre-seed program from First Round Capital
- **Antler:** Global residency-based program; builds companies from scratch
- **Founders Factory:** Industry-specific; strong corporate partnerships

### Vertical-Specific (surface based on idea category)
- **Fintech:** Plug and Play Fintech, Barclays Accelerator, Visa Everywhere Initiative
- **Healthtech:** Rock Health, StartX Med, Blueprint Health
- **Edtech:** Reach Capital, NewSchools Venture Fund, LearnLaunch
- **Cleantech:** Greentown Labs, Elemental Excelerator, DOE incubators
- **AI/ML:** NVIDIA Inception, Google for Startups, Microsoft for Startups
- **Retail/CPG:** Unilever Foundry, Target Accelerator, Techstars Retail

### Equity-Free Programs (always mention these)
- **Google for Startups:** Cloud credits ($200K+), mentorship, no equity
- **Microsoft for Startups (Founders Hub):** Azure credits ($150K+), GitHub, no equity
- **AWS Activate:** AWS credits up to $100K, no equity
- **NVIDIA Inception:** GPU compute credits, technical support, no equity
- **Stripe Atlas:** Not funding, but $50K in partner benefits + streamlined incorporation

## 7. HOW TO SURFACE THIS IN REPORTS

When writing Section 8 of an Idea Intelligence Report:

1. **Search first** — confirm current program availability and amounts (these change)
2. **Filter for relevance** — only include programs genuinely applicable to this idea and founder
3. **Lead with the highest-value opportunities** — not an exhaustive list, a curated one
4. **Always include at least one Smart Move** — a non-obvious strategic advantage the user likely hasn't considered
5. **Flag eligibility uncertainty** — if a program might apply but eligibility is unclear, say: *"You may qualify for [X] — worth a 15-minute eligibility check before applying."*
6. **Never recommend illegal strategies** — all Jugaad-style recommendations must be legal, ethical, and verifiable
