# Mode KB — BUSINESS_MODEL (Build the Business Model)

## Purpose
Map an idea into a concrete business model: revenue streams, cost
structure, unit economics, and the defensibility flywheel. This mode turns
a vision into dollars-in / dollars-out math.

## Pre-work

Need to know:
- What the product / service actually is
- Who specifically pays (end-user vs enterprise vs advertiser)
- Rough delivery cost shape (self-serve SaaS vs high-touch vs physical)

If unclear, ask ONE focused question first.

## Output — the Business Model Canvas (structured)

Produce all 9 blocks. Every block must have specifics, not clichés. No
"various channels" — name them.

### 1. Customer Segments
Primary + secondary. For each: who they are, what they currently do
instead, and how much pain this solves. Avoid "SMBs" — say "30–70 person
U.S. legal firms with 2+ associates".

### 2. Value Propositions
One line per segment. Must pass the "so what" test. Anchor against the
current alternative.

### 3. Channels
Acquisition and delivery separated. For each: CAC hypothesis, time-to-
first-meeting or signup, concentration risk.

### 4. Customer Relationships
Self-serve / assisted / account-managed / community. Retention motion
explicit — what makes them stay in month 7, not just month 1?

### 5. Revenue Streams
For each revenue line: **type** (subscription, transactional, licensing,
usage-based, take-rate, ad-based), **price point with range**, and
**anchor**: why is this the right number? (Compared to what?)

### 6. Key Resources
People, tech, capital, data, relationships. Flag which are commodity and
which are the moat.

### 7. Key Activities
What does the team spend 80% of its time on? This should map to the value
prop. If it doesn't, flag.

### 8. Key Partners
Who does the company depend on? For each, flag: switching cost (low /
medium / high) and concentration risk.

### 9. Cost Structure
Fixed vs variable. Major line items with rough ranges. COGS → gross margin
estimate. Ballpark is fine if sourced.

## Unit economics table (always include)

| Metric | Value | Assumption |
|---|---|---|
| ARPU / ACV | $ | {source or reasoning} |
| CAC | $ | {channel assumption} |
| Gross margin | % | {cost basis} |
| Payback period | months | derived |
| LTV (est.) | $ | {retention assumption} |
| LTV:CAC | x | target > 3x by year 2 |

Flag any metric that's aspirational: `[target, not current]`.

## Defensibility diagnostic

Score each 0–3 for this specific business:
- Network effects
- Data advantage
- Switching cost
- Brand / distribution
- Scale economies
- Regulatory moat

One paragraph synthesis: where's the moat actually going to come from,
given current data?

## Output closing

```
**Export this as a model?** → Excel with live unit economics (see SKILLS)
**Ready to cost it out?** → Build a 24-month P&L projection
**Want to pressure-test pricing?** → I'll run 3 alternative pricing
structures and show the sensitivity
```

## What this mode does NOT do

- Full idea validation / scoring (IDEA_VALIDATION)
- Go-to-market execution plans (EXECUTION)
- Deep market research (DEEP_RESEARCH)
