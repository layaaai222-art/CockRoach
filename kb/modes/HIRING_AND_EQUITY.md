# Mode KB — HIRING_AND_EQUITY (Build the team, set equity right)

## Purpose
Help the founder answer the four hiring questions that compound: **who
to hire next**, **employee vs contractor**, **what equity to grant**,
and **what vesting structure**. Equity decisions are mostly one-way
doors; this mode treats them with the gravity they deserve.

## Pre-work — required before substantive work

1. **Stage** — pre-product, post-launch, post-PMF, scaling
2. **Cash position** — runway in months at current burn
3. **Last raise** — informs the option pool size + dilution tolerance
4. **Founder count + current equity split**
5. **What gap is the user trying to fill** — engineering, design,
   sales, customer success, ops, content, ML

If "I just need help" without a defined gap — stop. Diagnose first.

## Decision 1 — Who to hire next? (the sequencing question)

The 5 most-debated first hires, ordered for typical SaaS:

| Stage | First 5 hires (in this order) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-product | (don't hire — find a co-founder) | Equity should reflect risk |
| Pre-PMF | Senior engineer #2 → designer → engineer #3 → first ops → first GTM | Build speed first; ship faster |
| Post-launch | First AE / customer success → engineer → designer → marketing | Revenue motion now matters |
| Post-PMF | Head of {weakest function} → VPs of remaining functions | Scale via leadership |

**The trap:** hiring a "head of X" before there's a function to head.
Don't hire a VP Marketing before you have 1 marketer doing the work.

**Contractor vs employee** decision rule:
- **Contractor** when: short-term, well-defined scope, no IP risk,
  needs <20 hrs/wk, you can swap them without business risk
- **Employee** when: long-term, ambiguous scope, deep IP, needs equity
  to align, founding-team-adjacent

**Cost reality:** an employee at $100K salary actually costs ~$130K with
employer taxes, benefits, software, equipment, and on-cost of equity.
Budget accordingly.

## Decision 2 — Equity grants (the FAST + 2026 norm framework)

### Founders + employees: 4-year vest, 1-year cliff (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**Standard schedule:**
- Total grant vests over 4 years
- 1-year "cliff" — if person leaves before 12 months, gets 0
- After cliff, 25% vests; remainder vests monthly over the next 36 months

**Why non-negotiable:** without vesting, a co-founder who leaves at
month 3 keeps 25% of your company. That kills future fundraising. Every
investor will require it; do it preemptively.

### Employee equity bands by stage

| Hire # | Stage | Senior eng / first ICs | Mid-level | Junior |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1–3 | Pre-PMF | 1.0–3.0% | 0.5–1.0% | 0.1–0.3% |
| 4–10 | Pre-PMF | 0.5–1.0% | 0.2–0.5% | 0.05–0.15% |
| 11–25 | Post-launch | 0.2–0.5% | 0.1–0.2% | 0.02–0.05% |
| 26–50 | Post-PMF | 0.1–0.25% | 0.05–0.1% | 0.01–0.025% |
| 51+ | Scaling | 0.05–0.1% | 0.02–0.05% | <0.01% |

**Option pool size:** typical 10% pre-seed, 12–15% pre-Series-A
top-up, 5–10% per subsequent round. The first 10 employees collectively
get ~10% of the company in stock options.

### Advisors: FAST framework

The Founder/Advisor Standard Template (FAST) — by Founder Institute,
adopted across 10K+ startups — is the de-facto standard:

| Company stage | Standard advisor | Strategic | Expert (monthly engagement, recruits, takes calls) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Idea | 0.25% | 0.5% | **1.0%** |
| Startup (post-MVP) | 0.20% | 0.33% | **0.67%** |
| Growth (PMF + revenue) | 0.10% | 0.20% | **0.40%** |

**Standard advisor terms:**
- 2-year vesting (vs 4-year for employees) — advisors deliver value early
- 3-month cliff (vs 1-year) — quick out if relationship sours
- Vests monthly after cliff
- Restricted stock or options, your call

### Co-founder equity split

**Defaults that work:**
- Equal split (50/50, 33/33/33) when contributions are roughly equal
- Slicing pie / dynamic equity (Slicing Pie by Mike Moyer) for asymmetric
- One-founder-2x rule: if one founder is the original ideator + works
  full-time + put in capital, 65/35 or 60/40 is reasonable

**Always include:**
- 4-year vesting on co-founder shares too
- Right of first refusal (ROFR) — if a co-founder leaves, company can
  buy back unvested shares
- IP assignment to the company (not the individual)

## Decision 3 — Vesting (the legal architecture)

**Standard employee schedule (4y/1y cliff):**
- Day 0: Stock options granted, $0 vested
- Day 364: Quit → 0 shares
- Day 365: 25% vests automatically (1,250 of 5,000 options)
- Day 366 onwards: ~104 options vest per month (3,750 / 36)
- Day 1,460 (4 years): All 5,000 vested

**Acceleration triggers (for senior hires):**
- **Single-trigger:** 100% vest on company sale (rare; only for
  founder-equivalent execs)
- **Double-trigger:** 100% vest on (sale AND acquirer terminating
  without cause) — much more common for execs / VPs

**Refresh grants:** plan an annual "refresh" of ~25% of original grant,
starting at year 2, to keep top performers from leaving for a fresh
4-year vest elsewhere.

**Early exercise:** for very-early hires, allow early exercise so they
can start the 83(b) tax clock — saves them 5-figure tax liability if
the company succeeds. File the 83(b) election within 30 days of grant
or it's lost.

## Decision 4 — Sourcing + screening

**Pre-PMF sourcing:**
1. Founder's network (10–20 reach-outs)
2. Twitter/LinkedIn job posts (if founder has audience)
3. Y Combinator's WorkAtAStartup, Wellfound (formerly AngelList)
4. Specialist communities (Tech engineering Discord/Slack, Latitudes for
   remote-first roles, Otta for product/design)
5. Targeted outbound via LinkedIn Sales Navigator

**Screening flow that wastes least founder time:**
1. **30-min intro call** — culture/why-this-startup; you're filtering
   for genuine interest, not just "any job"
2. **Take-home / paid trial** (3–6 hrs paid; ICs only) — real work
3. **Technical / functional deep-dive** (1 hr; live)
4. **Founder + team chat** (1 hr; mutual fit)
5. **Reference checks** (3 refs; ask: "would you hire them again?
   what's their dev area?")
6. **Offer** — same day or next day after refs check out

**Red flags (any one is a hard pass):**
- Bad-mouthing previous employers
- Asking about title before scope
- "Where will I be in 3 years?" question (wrong stage)
- Slow / hostile take-home delivery
- Reference checks come back tepid (not negative — just tepid)

## Output structure

When the user asks "what should I offer X" or "who do I hire next":

```
## Hiring recommendation — {project name}

### TL;DR
- Next hire: {role}
- Type: {employee / contractor}
- Comp: ${X}K cash + {Y}% equity
- Vesting: {4y/1y cliff standard}
- Confidence: {high/medium/low}

### Sequencing rationale
{Why this role next, vs the alternatives the user considered}

### Comp benchmarks
| Source | Cash band | Equity band |
|---|---|---|
| Levels.fyi (matched seniority + stage) | $X-Y | A%-B% |
| Carta data (matched stage) | $X-Y | A%-B% |
| AngelList / Wellfound (matched role) | $X-Y | A%-B% |

### Offer recommendation
- Cash: ${X}K (band: ${A}-${B}K)
- Equity: {Y}% in stock options ({Z} of {pool size} pool)
- Vesting: 4-year, 1-year cliff
- Refresh expectation: ~25% of grant at year 2

### Sourcing plan (top 3 channels)
1. {channel} — {N expected candidates / wk}
2. ...

### Screening rubric
- {3-5 must-haves}
- {2-3 nice-to-haves}
- {1-2 hard fails}

### Decision-log entry suggestion
Category: hiring
Reversibility: **expensive** (firing is bad; equity grants can be
clawed back via vesting but the hire imposes culture cost)
Pre-mortem: "Wrong if {biggest assumption — usually 'they'll grow
into the role'}"
Revisit: 90-day check-in
```

## Producing artifacts

- **Job description** — role, expectations, comp band, equity grant,
  vesting, our story; render via Skills KB to PDF or DOCX
- **Offer letter** — clean template (recommend lawyer-review for
  jurisdiction); decline to provide as legal-binding doc
- **Cap table impact preview** — XLSX with hire's grant + dilution
  effect on founders / option pool
- **Reference-check script** — 5 standard questions, role-customized

## What this mode does NOT do

- Background checks / drug screens — recommend Checkr, GoodHire
- Payroll / benefits administration — recommend Gusto, Rippling, Deel
- Visa / immigration — recommend a specialist immigration attorney
- Compliance (EEO, harassment training) — recommend HR-as-a-service like
  Justworks or Sequoia HR

## Handoff suggestions

- "Draft the JD?" → JD via Skills KB; user reviews and posts
- "Build the offer letter?" → produce template, flag legal-review need
- "Show cap table impact?" → XLSX with this grant added
- "Log as a decision?" → Decision form, reversibility=expensive
- "Plan first 90 days?" → EXECUTION mode for the hire's onboarding plan
