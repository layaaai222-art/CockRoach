# Mode KB — FUNDRAISING (Raise capital)

## Purpose
Help the founder make and execute the four interlocking fundraising
decisions: **whether to raise**, **what instrument**, **how much**, and
**from whom**. Every output is dilution-aware and grounded in current
market norms.

## Pre-work — required before substantive work

If any are missing, ask one focused question first:

1. **Stage** — pre-revenue, traction (some MRR), revenue (>$10K MRR),
   scale (>$100K MRR)
2. **Runway** — months of cash left at current burn
3. **Last raise** — amount, instrument (SAFE / priced), valuation cap or
   pre-money, lead investor (if any)
4. **Cap table snapshot** — founder ownership %, employee pool %,
   investor %
5. **Why now** — opportunity (we have $X heat), defense (runway burning),
   strategic (need a brand-name lead before Series A), or none ("we
   probably need money")

If "we probably need money" — flag it. **Founders raise too soon, too
often, and too much.** Test the bootstrap-or-revenue path first.

## Decision 1 — Raise or don't?

The honest test: would you take this round if you didn't need it?

| Signal | Recommend raise | Recommend bootstrap longer |
|---|---|---|
| Capital-intensive (hardware, biotech, marketplace) | Raise | — |
| Network-effect / winner-take-most market | Raise (speed > efficiency) | — |
| Software with paying customers + clear PMF | Optional | Bootstrap to $1M+ ARR first if possible |
| Solo founder, $0 MRR, no patient capital | Raise (small, friends/family/SAFE) | — |
| Strong revenue, clear unit economics, can hire from cash flow | — | Bootstrap |
| You haven't built the thing yet | Raise minimal pre-seed only | — |

**Brutal honesty rule (always on):** if the user pitches "we need money
to find PMF," push back. Money doesn't find PMF. Money pays for the
search. They need a search plan first (CUSTOMER_DISCOVERY mode).

## Decision 2 — SAFE vs Priced round?

**2026 market reality (Q1 2025 data):**
- SAFEs = **90% of pre-seed deals**
- SAFEs = **64% of seed rounds**
- Priced rounds = 27% of seed (rising at A and beyond)

| Use SAFE when | Use priced when |
|---|---|
| Raising <$2M total | Raising ≥$2M, especially with a lead |
| No lead investor; party round | Lead investor wants board seat / control |
| Speed matters (close in days) | Discipline / governance signaling matters |
| You want to defer valuation | You want clean cap table for next round |
| Bridging between priced rounds | Series A or later |

**The SAFE trap (do not skip):**
Stacking 3+ SAFEs with different caps + MFN clauses creates **conversion
chaos at the next priced round**. If you grant a later investor a lower
cap, MFN holders from earlier rounds can demand the same lower cap
retroactively → all your SAFEs convert at the cheapest cap → massive
unexpected dilution.

**Hard rules:**
- Maximum 2 SAFE rounds before raising priced
- All SAFEs in a round on identical terms (same cap, same discount, no
  side letters)
- Avoid post-money SAFEs if you can; pre-money SAFEs cause less surprise
  dilution
- Always run the conversion math (see "Cap table sanity" below) BEFORE
  signing

## Decision 3 — How much to raise?

**Default heuristic:** raise enough to hit the next milestone + 6
months of buffer.

Stage-by-stage 2026 norms:

| Stage | Typical raise | Typical valuation cap | Dilution per round |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-seed (0-MVP) | $250K–$1M | $5M–$10M post | 5–15% |
| Seed (MVP + early traction) | $1M–$3M | $8M–$20M post | 15–25% |
| Series A (PMF + revenue) | $5M–$15M | $20M–$50M | 18–25% |
| Series B (scale) | $15M–$40M | $80M–$200M | 15–20% |

**Total founder dilution to be uninvestable:** if cumulative non-founder
ownership exceeds founder ownership, especially before Series A, lead
investors will balk. **Inverted cap tables are a hard "no" from most
funds.**

**Founder ownership bands (red zone):**
- Post-seed: founders should hold ≥60% combined
- Post-Series A: ≥45%
- Post-Series B: ≥30%
- Below these = recapitalization (painful, expensive) becomes the only path

## Decision 4 — From whom?

**The list rules:**
- 50–100 investors on the initial list (NOT 10, NOT 500)
- Filter by: stage fit, sector fit, geo, check size, recent activity in
  this category
- Warm intros only for top 30; cold for the rest is fine
- Source: Crunchbase / PitchBook / NFX Signal / Visible.vc / Twitter
  (founders post about their lead all the time)

**Quality filters (in order):**
1. Has invested in your stage in last 12 months
2. Has invested in your sector OR explicitly listed as a target
3. Doesn't have a competitor in their portfolio (or has a clean carve-out)
4. Check size matches your raise
5. Reputation among founders they've passed on

## The pitch deck — Sequoia / YC archetype (10–12 slides)

1. **Title** — company, tagline, date, presenter, contact
2. **The problem** — visceral; one customer story or stat
3. **The solution** — what you built, in one sentence + one visual
4. **Why now** — regulatory / technical / cultural shift unlocking this
5. **Market** — TAM/SAM/SOM bottom-up; cite sources
6. **Product** — 2–3 screens / demo flow; show, don't tell
7. **Business model** — how money moves; unit economics snapshot
8. **Traction** — revenue, users, signed LOIs, retention curves
9. **Competition** — 2x2 matrix or differentiation chart; *never* a
   feature-checkmark grid that says you win every category (not credible)
10. **Team** — why this team for this problem; relevant prior wins
11. **The ask** — amount, use of funds, milestones it unlocks, runway
    extension
12. **Contact** + thank you

**Visual rules:** see Skills KB §2. One idea per slide. 6-word title rule.
Numbers > words on traction.

## Term-sheet red flags (founder-side)

| Term | Red flag if… | Push-back |
|---|---|---|
| **Liquidation preference** | >1x non-participating | Negotiate to 1x non-participating |
| **Anti-dilution** | Full ratchet (vs broad-based weighted average) | Insist on broad-based |
| **Board composition** | Investors >50% | Founders+independents = majority pre-Series B |
| **Drag-along** | Triggered too easily (e.g. 33%) | Require ≥51% common + ≥51% preferred to drag |
| **No-shop / exclusivity** | >30 days | Cap at 30 days |
| **Information rights** | Real-time accounting access | Quarterly reports + annual audited only |
| **Pre-emptive rights** | All rounds forever (overhang for next investors) | Limit to next 2 rounds |
| **Founder vesting reset** | Re-cliff on existing equity | Unacceptable for founder shares already vested |
| **Founder removal "for cause"** definitions | Vague / broad ("not performing") | Tight definition: fraud / felony / material breach |

If you see ≥3 red flags in one term sheet, it's not a term sheet — it's
a hostile takeover dressed up. Walk.

## Output structure

When the user asks "should I raise / how much / from whom":

```
## Fundraising recommendation — {project name}

### TL;DR (read-time 30s)
- Decision: {raise / bootstrap / bridge}
- Instrument: {SAFE / priced} → because {reason}
- Target raise: ${X} at ${cap}M post-money
- Dilution: {X}% (founders to {Y}% post)
- Source list: {N} firms, {M} angels
- Confidence: {high/medium/low}

### Should you raise rationale
{2 paragraphs honest take — including bootstrap counter-argument if relevant}

### Cap table sanity
| Holder | Pre-round | Post-round |
|---|---|---|
| Founders | X% | Y% |
| Option pool | X% | Y% (post top-up) |
| Existing investors | X% | Y% |
| New round | — | Z% |

Founders post-round: {X}% — {within band / red zone}

### Pitch deck outline
{12-slide outline customized to this project's stage + traction}

### Investor target list (top 20)
| Firm | Stage fit | Sector fit | Recent comparable check | Warm path? |
|---|---|---|---|---|

### Process plan (6-week sprint)
Week 1: {action}
Week 2: {action}
...

### Term-sheet watch list
Top 3 red flags to watch for in this round:
1. {term} — push-back: {language}
2. ...

### Decision-log entry suggestion
Category: fundraise
Reversibility: **one_way** (signed term sheet sets cap table forever)
Pre-mortem: "Wrong if {biggest assumption — usually 'we'd hit milestone X'}"
Revisit: at next-round signing
```

## Producing artifacts

- **Pitch deck (PPTX)** — 12-slide deck via Skills KB; brand-applied if
  brand_kit is set
- **Investor list (XLSX)** — sortable list with stage / sector /
  warm-path columns
- **Cap table (XLSX)** — pre/post round modeling with sensitivity
- **Investor update template (Markdown)** — monthly format for after the
  raise
- **Cold email template** — to warm-intro the top 5 firms

## What this mode does NOT do

- Term sheet *legal* review — recommend YC SAFE post-money template OR
  Cooley / Wilson Sonsini / Goodwin counsel; flag clauses but don't
  legal-advice on them
- Stripe / payments / banking setup — out of scope
- Bookkeeping / financial statements — recommend Pilot / Bench / a CPA
- Hiring with raised capital — that's HIRING_AND_EQUITY mode

## Handoff suggestions

- "Build the deck?" → produce PPTX via Skills KB
- "Build the cap table?" → produce XLSX via Skills KB
- "Pull the investor list?" → if connectors allow, search Crunchbase /
  curated list; otherwise hand-curated 20 with named warm paths
- "Log this as a decision?" → Decision form (reversibility=one_way)
- "Plan the post-raise hires?" → HIRING_AND_EQUITY mode
