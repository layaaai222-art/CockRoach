# Framework — Dream 100 (Chet Holmes / Russell Brunson)

## Origin
Chet Holmes, *The Ultimate Sales Machine* (2007); popularized for
digital-era founders by Russell Brunson, *Traffic Secrets* (2020).
The premise: instead of trying to reach *millions* of customers
through cold scale, identify the **100 places, people, or accounts
that already have your dream customers' attention** — and earn
your way in.

## When to use
- B2B with high ACV ($5k+) and concentrated buying
- Creator / community-driven products (audiences are the moat)
- Pre-launch — building a beachhead distribution surface
- Replacing/augmenting cold-paid with warm-distribution
- ABM (Account-Based Marketing) for enterprise sales

## When NOT to use
- High-volume DTC < $50 AOV (you need cold scale; Dream 100 is too
  slow)
- Truly horizontal SaaS where every SMB is a customer (channel
  fragmentation makes the "100" impossible to define)

## The two flavors

### B2C / creator-driven Dream 100 (Brunson)
Identify the **100 places where your dream customers already
congregate** under someone else's banner:
- 30 podcasts they listen to
- 20 YouTube channels / Substacks they follow
- 20 communities (Slack / Discord / Reddit / FB groups)
- 15 newsletters they subscribe to
- 15 events / conferences / Twitter accounts they orbit

You don't try to *be* the next 100 — you **partner with, sponsor,
guest-on, or collaborate with** the 100. Their attention becomes
yours through borrowed credibility.

### B2B / ABM Dream 100 (Holmes)
Identify the **100 accounts** that, if signed, would transform the
business — by revenue, logo prestige, expansion potential, or
network effect. Then concentrate sales effort on those 100, not
on a list of 10,000.

## The criteria (B2B)

For B2B Dream 100, score each candidate account on:
1. **ACV potential** — top quartile of your pricing
2. **Strategic fit** — they need exactly what you do, not "kind of"
3. **Logo value** — would adding them to your homepage close other
   deals?
4. **Champion likelihood** — is there a known buyer/champion
   reachable?
5. **Expansion math** — does seat 1 → 100 happen here?

Score 1-5 each, total 5-25. Only accounts ≥ 18 make the list.

## The 4-touch / 6-month rhythm (Holmes)

> Holmes's research: it takes ~6 months and 4-12 deliberate touches
> to convert a Dream 100 account from cold to a meeting.

**Per-account quarterly cadence:**
- Month 1: Personalized email + LinkedIn connect
- Month 2: Value-first asset (industry research, audit, intro to
  someone they'd want to meet)
- Month 3: Direct mail / hand-written note / video message
- Month 4: Event meet (you go where they are)
- Month 5-6: Personal connection through Dream 100 partner
  (mutual contact, conference booth)

Track every touch per account in a CRM. Rotate angles; never
repeat the same outreach.

## The B2C / creator playbook (Brunson)

### Phase 1 — Map (Month 1)
Identify all 100 platforms/people/communities. Rank by audience
size × audience fit × accessibility.

### Phase 2 — Buy your way in (Month 2-4)
Sponsor / pay / advertise on the most-accessible 30. Buy your way
into proximity.

### Phase 3 — Earn your way in (Month 4-12)
Guest podcasts. Partner content. Co-create. Newsletter swaps.
Conference panels. The 70 you can't buy, you earn.

### Phase 4 — Become one (Year 2+)
Build your own audience asset (newsletter, podcast, community).
Now you are part of someone else's Dream 100.

## Common traps

- **Quantity over quality**: 1000 lukewarm accounts beats 100 cold
  ones. False — Dream 100 says the opposite. 100 perfectly-fit
  beats 1000 random.
- **All buy, no earn**: paying for sponsorships gets noticed once;
  earning trust builds compounding distribution.
- **Generic outreach**: if your email could be sent to any of the
  100, it will be ignored by all of them. Personalize.
- **Wrong list**: the 100 are who *your customers* listen to, not
  who *you* admire.

## Step-by-step

1. **Define your dream customer** in one sentence (segment + ICP).
2. **For B2B**: list candidate accounts. Score 1-5 on the 5
   criteria. Filter to top 100 (or 50 for early-stage).
3. **For B2C / creator**: list 30 podcasts, 20 YT, 20 communities,
   15 newsletters, 15 events that your dream customer orbits.
4. **Build the touch sequence** — 4-6 months, 4-12 touches.
5. **Track in CRM / Notion / spreadsheet** with last-touch +
   next-touch + relationship-stage.
6. **Review monthly.** Move accounts up the ladder: cold → warm →
   meeting → opportunity → customer / partnership / collaboration.
